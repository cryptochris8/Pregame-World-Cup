# Atlanta Bar/Restaurant Outreach — Email Package

**Created 2026-05-22.** Cold outreach to hand-picked Atlanta venue owners to recruit them as $499 Pregame venue partners ahead of the 8 Mercedes-Benz Stadium matches (Jun 15 – Jul 15).

---

## How to use this

1. Pick 10 venues (you said you'd hand-pick — good).
2. For each, write **one** specific personalized line and slot it into `[PERSONAL OBSERVATION]`. This is the single most important field — generic emails get deleted. Examples below.
3. Send Tuesday or Thursday, **9:00–10:30 AM** or **2:00–4:00 PM** Atlanta time (pre-open admin window and the lull between lunch and dinner). Never lunch or dinner rush.
4. Send from a real `chris@pregameworldcup.com`-style address, **not** `info@` or `support@` — owners trust founder-to-founder.
5. Cap follow-ups at **4 total touches over ~3 weeks** then stop.

---

## Subject lines (pick one — A/B if you want)

All lowercase, no emoji, no caps, 4–7 words. Five-word range hits best response per current B2B data.

- `quick idea for [Bar Name] in june`
- `8 matches at the benz — your patio?`
- `watch parties at [Bar Name] this summer`

## Tone & vocabulary cheat-sheet (so you sound local)

**Use:** match, soccer, kickoff, supporters, watch party, patio, USMNT, "the Benz," 5-Stripes, ATL UTD.
**Avoid:** footy, the beautiful game, premier viewing experience, fan engagement platform, "synergy," "I hope this email finds you well," "circle back."

**One judgment call:** The follow-up #2 link goes to `pregame-b089e.web.app/venue/signup` — fine to use, but the prettier URL `pregameworldcup.com/venues` (which is the new For Venues landing page on the marketing site, with links into signup) might convert better as the public face. Pick one and be consistent across the sequence.

**One IP call:** I used "the tournament's trademark rules" (not "FIFA's") in the primary email — keeps it generic and consistent with the rest of Pregame's positioning. Owners will know what you mean. If you want to be more direct in 1:1 sales, swap to "FIFA's trademark rules" — it's a cold email, not a code string, so the strict no-FIFA app rule doesn't apply.
