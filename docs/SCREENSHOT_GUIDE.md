# App Store Screenshot Guide — Pregame World Cup

**Purpose:** Create a complete new set of screenshots for the App Store submission reflecting the current dark theme UI and feature set.

**Required sizes:**
- 6.7-inch iPhone (iPhone 15 Pro Max / 16 Pro Max) — **Required**
- 6.5-inch iPhone (iPhone 14 Plus / 15 Plus) — Recommended
- iPad Pro 12.9-inch — If supporting iPad

**Apple Guidelines:**
- Screenshots must reflect the app **in use** (not splash/login screens as primary)
- No marketing materials that don't reflect the actual UI
- Highlight the app's core concept and value

---

## Recommended Screenshot Set (10 screenshots, priority order)

### 1. Match Schedule (World Cup > Matches tab)
**What to capture:** The match schedule list showing upcoming World Cup matches with team flags, dates, and venues.
**Why:** This is the app's primary screen — shows the core value proposition at a glance.
**Navigation:** Open app > World Cup tab > Matches sub-tab

### 2. AI Match Prediction / Match Detail
**What to capture:** A match detail page showing the AI-powered prediction with win probabilities, key factors, and analysis.
**Why:** This is the app's signature feature — AI predictions are the main differentiator.
**Navigation:** World Cup > Matches > Tap any match

### 3. Group Standings
**What to capture:** Group standings table showing teams with points, W-D-L, goal difference. Pick a competitive group (e.g., Group I with France/Argentina or Group L with England/Croatia).
**Why:** Essential World Cup content — fans constantly check standings.
**Navigation:** World Cup > Groups sub-tab > Select a group

### 4. Team Detail Page
**What to capture:** A popular team's detail page (e.g., USA, Brazil, or England) showing the confederation gradient header, world ranking, World Cup titles, and stats cards.
**Why:** Shows depth of team information and the polished dark theme design.
**Navigation:** World Cup > Teams > Tap a team

### 5. Player Profile
**What to capture:** A star player's profile (e.g., Messi, Mbappe, or Pulisic) showing career stats, caps, goals, playing style.
**Why:** Demonstrates the rich player database.
**Navigation:** World Cup > Players > Tap a player

### 6. Manager Detail
**What to capture:** A manager's profile showing tactical approach, record, honors, and World Cup outlook.
**Why:** Unique feature — most apps don't have detailed manager profiles.
**Navigation:** World Cup > Managers > Tap a manager

### 7. Venue Detail
**What to capture:** A host venue page (e.g., MetLife Stadium or SoFi Stadium) showing capacity, location, and match schedule.
**Why:** Shows the venue discovery feature for fans planning to attend.
**Navigation:** From a match detail > Tap the venue, or navigate through Venues

### 8. Bracket / Knockout Stage
**What to capture:** The knockout bracket view showing the tournament progression (Round of 32 through Final).
**Why:** Visual bracket is engaging and shows tournament structure.
**Navigation:** World Cup > Bracket sub-tab (if available) or from standings

### 9. User Profile / Social Features
**What to capture:** A user profile page showing predictions, favorites, and social stats.
**Why:** Shows the community/social dimension of the app.
**Navigation:** Profile tab (bottom nav, rightmost)

### 10. Activity Feed
**What to capture:** The social activity feed showing posts, predictions, and fan interactions.
**Why:** Demonstrates the social/community features.
**Navigation:** Activity Feed tab (bottom nav, 2nd from left)

---

## Screenshots to AVOID

- Login/signup screen (Apple says these don't show the app "in use")
- Splash screen or loading states
- Empty states with no data
- Settings screens
- EULA/Terms acceptance (functional but not a showcase)
- Error screens

---

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Use demo account data** — Make sure the reviewer account has predictions, favorites, and activity
2. **Dark theme is default** — All screenshots should show the dark theme (deep blue/slate background)
3. **Show populated screens** — Every screenshot should have real data visible
4. **Landscape orientation** — Not needed; the app is portrait-only
5. **No status bar clutter** — Take screenshots in a clean state (full battery icon, clean time, no notification badges from other apps)
6. **Device:** Use an actual iPhone 15 Pro Max or 16 Pro Max for the 6.7" screenshots
7. **Consider adding brief text overlays** in App Store Connect (not in the screenshots themselves) to highlight key features
