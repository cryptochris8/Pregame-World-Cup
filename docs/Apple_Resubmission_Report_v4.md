## What Was Different This Time

For the first time in seven rejections, the Review Team provided a screenshot identifying the specific content in question. The screenshot showed our onboarding screen with the subtitle:

> "Your ultimate companion for the 2026 World Cup."

We are grateful for the clarity. This report documents how we have addressed this and every related occurrence throughout the app, the metadata, and the marketing website.

---

## Resolution: Comprehensive User-Visible Scrub

We have removed every user-visible reference to the trademarked tournament name from the binary, platform metadata, localizations, and marketing surface.

### App binary

| Surface | Before | After |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Onboarding subtitle (the flagged screen) | "Your ultimate companion for the 2026 World Cup." | **"Your ultimate companion for soccer's biggest summer."** |
| App display name on Home Screen | Pregame WC26 | **Pregame** |
| In-app app bar titles | World Cup 2026 | **2026 Tournament** |
| Navigation tab | World Cup | **Tournament** |
| Stat labels | World Cup Titles, World Cup Goals, World Cup Appearances | **Tournament Titles, Tournament Goals, Tournament Appearances** |
| Share text | Check out {name} on Pregame World Cup | **Check out {name} on Pregame** |
| Deep-link meta titles | {home} vs {away} - World Cup 2026 | **{home} vs {away} - 2026 tournament** |
| Rivalry descriptions, storylines, historical references | "World Cup history / finals / moments / rivalries" | **"tournament history / finals / moments / rivalries"** |

**Scope:** 151 files modified across `lib/`, `ios/`, `android/`, and `pubspec.yaml`. 664 insertions, 667 deletions. Every occurrence verified via automated grep pass; zero user-visible residuals remain.

### iOS platform metadata (`Info.plist`)

All 7 user-visible system permission prompts rewritten to say just "Pregame" and reference "the 2026 tournament" / "tournament matches" instead of the trademarked name. `CFBundleDisplayName` and `CFBundleName` both changed from "Pregame WC26" to **"Pregame"**.

### Android platform metadata (`strings.xml`)

`app_name`, `widget_name`, and `widget_description` all rewritten to neutral language.

### Marketing website (`pregameworldcup.com`)

45 user-visible "World Cup" references across `index.html`, `support.html`, `privacy.html`, `terms.html`, and the App Store review materials page (`review/index.html`) all scrubbed. Page titles, meta descriptions, Open Graph tags, Twitter card metadata, hero text, feature descriptions, CTAs, and footers all neutralized. Redeployed on Netlify concurrent with this submission.

### App Store Connect metadata

## What We Preserved

- **Class names, data-model field names, and Firestore collection names** that contain "worldCup" (e.g., `WorldCupMatch` as a Dart type, `worldcup_matches` as a Firestore collection). These are internal implementation identifiers that are never rendered to users. We use them in the same way a news app might name its internal class `WorldCupArticle` without that identifier ever appearing in the user interface.
- **Code comments.** 79 `// World Cup ...` style comments remain in the source for developer context. These are not compiled into the shipping binary and are not visible to users.

## Content Independence (Reiterated)
